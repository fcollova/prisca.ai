The Problem

Every Prompt You Send to a Cloud AI Gets Logged

When your team uses ChatGPT, Gemini, or any cloud AI service, something invisible happens: your data travels. It leaves your firewall, crosses external infrastructure, and lands on servers you do not control. Most enterprise AI agreements include data retention clauses — sometimes for months.

“For confidential contracts, HR data, proprietary source code, or client strategies — sending that data to a cloud AI is simply unacceptable.”

For law firms, medical practices, financial advisors, engineering teams, and any company handling sensitive information, this creates real exposure: regulatory risk, competitive risk, and reputational risk under GDPR and the incoming EU AI Act.

The solution is not to ban AI. It is to own it.

What Is Private Corporate AI?

Private Corporate AI is a complete, self-hosted AI stack that runs entirely inside your infrastructure. Think of it as building your own private ChatGPT — one that reads and reasons over your company documents, answers questions from your knowledge base, and never sends a single byte outside your network.

It is built on battle-tested open source components, wired together into a production-ready system you can deploy in under 30 minutes.

Under the Hood

The RAG Pipeline: Not Just Another Chatbot

Most "chat with your docs" tools are simple: embed your text, do a cosine similarity search, stuff results into a prompt. Private Corporate AI goes further with two techniques that meaningfully improve answer quality.

Contextual Retrieval

When a document gets indexed, the system does not just split it into chunks. For each chunk, the local LLM generates a short contextual prefix based on the entire document. This prevents the most common RAG failure: a retrieved chunk that is meaningless without its surrounding context.

Example

A chunk might read: "the party shall pay within 30 days." Without context, which party? Which clause? Contextual Retrieval automatically prepends: "From Section 4.2 of the Service Agreement regarding payment terms:" — now the chunk is self-contained.

Hybrid Search — Dense + Sparse + RRF

The system runs two searches in parallel: semantic vector search that understands meaning, and BM25 lexical search that catches exact terms, product codes, and acronyms. Results are fused via Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF).

Performance

On technical enterprise documents, hybrid search delivers 30–40% better recall compared to semantic search alone — capturing both conceptually related content and exact terminology matches.

In LITE mode, quantized 4-bit models (q4_K_M) retain ~97% of full-precision quality while requiring 60% less RAM. A 7B model fits comfortably in 4–5 GB of RAM and runs on any modern server CPU.

Getting Started

One Command to Rule Them All

The entire installation — Docker setup, model download, SSL certificates, credential generation, and branding — is handled by a single interactive script.

bash
git clone https://github.com/fcollova/Private-Corporate-AI.git
cd Private-Corporate-AI
chmod +x install.sh
sudo ./install.sh

The wizard detects your hardware, guides you through model selection, asks for your company name and color theme, generates unique credentials and SSL certificates, and spins everything up. Five to fifteen minutes later — mostly waiting for model downloads — you have a running private AI.

For automated deployments, non-interactive flags are available:

bash
sudo ./install.sh --gpu   # Force GPU mode
sudo ./install.sh --cpu   # Force CPU-only mode

Security

Security: Not an Afterthought

Network isolation. Two separate Docker networks — frontend_net and backend_net. The LLM engine and vector database are never directly reachable from the browser layer. Traffic can only flow through the RAG Backend.

Single entry point. Only ports 80 and 443 are exposed to the host. Qdrant (6333), Ollama (11434), and the RAG Backend (8000) are internal only, unreachable from outside.

Randomized credentials. Every installation generates unique QDRANT_API_KEY and WEBUI_SECRET_KEY values. No default passwords ship with the project.

bash — production firewall
sudo ufw allow 443/tcp
sudo ufw deny 6333/tcp   # Qdrant — internal only
sudo ufw deny 11434/tcp  # Ollama — internal only
sudo ufw enable

Who Is This For?

Private Corporate AI is designed for organizations that need AI capabilities but cannot — or will not — send their data to cloud providers:

  • Law firms and legal departments — contracts, case files, legal research
  • Medical practices and healthcare — clinical guidelines, compliance documents
  • Financial services — client portfolios, regulatory filings, internal policies
  • Engineering teams — internal wikis, codebases, technical documentation
